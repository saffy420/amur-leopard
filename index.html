The Exhibit

One body, three selves, a thinning gene pool.

The Amur leopard lives on a thin strip of Far East Russia. Every individual is a little different: coat density, rosette spacing, and body length, all shaped by wherever it was born. When a leopard gets poached it's not just one cat gone, it's a genotype that existed nowhere else. Traps set for deer catch leopards as bycatch, and pelts and bones still cross the border on black markets. With only around 120 adults left, even a few deaths a year push the species closer to a point it cannot come back from.
